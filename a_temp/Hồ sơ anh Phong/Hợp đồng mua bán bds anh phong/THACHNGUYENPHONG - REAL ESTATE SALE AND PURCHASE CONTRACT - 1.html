ARTICLE 13. FORCE MAJEURE 13.1. The Parties agree that one of the following cases shall be considered a Force Majeure Event: (a) Due to war or due to natural disasters or due to changes in State legal policies; (b) Due to the necessity to comply with decisions of competent State agencies or other cases as prescribed by law; (c) Due to accidents, illnesses requiring emergency treatment at medical facilities. 13.2. Pure financial difficulties shall not be considered a Force Majeure Event. 13.3. When one of the Force Majeure Events as agreed in Clause 1 of this Article occurs, the Party affected by the Force Majeure Event must notify the other Party in writing or directly within 15 (fifteen) days from the date of occurrence of the Force Majeure Event, accompanied by documents proving the reason for the force majeure (if any). The Party affected by the Force Majeure Event failing to perform its obligations shall not be considered in breach of the Agreement and this shall not be a basis for the other Party to terminate this Agreement. 13.4. The performance of obligations under the Agreement by the Parties shall be suspended during the occurrence of the Force Majeure Event. The Parties shall resume performance of their obligations after the Force Majeure Event ceases, except as provided in Clause 1 of Article 14 of this Agreement. 13.5. For clarity, the exemption from liability due to a Force Majeure Event under this Article shall not apply to obligations of a Party to pay money to the other Party under this Agreement. 13.6. In case of a Force Majeure Event, the term for performance of the Agreement shall be extended by the duration of the Force Majeure Event during which the affected Party cannot perform its obligations under the Agreement, and this shall be confirmed in writing by the Parties.
ARTICLE 14. TERMINATION OF THE AGREEMENT 14.1. Cases of termination of the Agreement: (a) The Parties agree to terminate the Agreement. In such case, the Parties shall prepare a written agreement specifying the conditions and timing of termination; (b) The Buyer delays payment of the Sale Price of the Resort Real Estate as agreed in Clause 1 Article 11 of this Agreement; (c) The Seller delays the handover of the Resort Real Estate as agreed in Article 5 of this Agreement; (d) In case the Party affected by the Force Majeure Event is unable to overcome it to continue performing its obligations within 90 (ninety) days from the date of occurrence of the Force Majeure Event, and the Parties do not have another agreement, then either Party shall have the right to unilaterally terminate this Agreement after notifying the other Party in writing at least 07 (seven) days in advance and such termination shall not be considered a breach of the Agreement. 14.2. Handling of consequences due to termination of the Agreement under Clause 1 of this Article as follows: (a) If this Agreement is terminated under Article 14.1(a), the consequences of termination shall be agreed in writing by the Parties; (b) If this Agreement is terminated under Article 14.1(b), the consequences of termination shall be handled according to the provisions of Article 11.1 of this Agreement; (c) If this Agreement is terminated under Article 14.1(c), the consequences of termination shall be handled according to the provisions of Article 11.2 of this Agreement; (d) If this Agreement is terminated under Article 14.1(d), the Parties agree that the Seller shall refund the amounts paid by the Buyer (excluding any accrued late payment penalties) to the Buyer up to the termination date of the Agreement without any interest, penalty or compensation for damages. 14.3. Except as otherwise provided in this Agreement, the Parties agree that no Party shall have the right to unilaterally terminate this Agreement for any reason.
